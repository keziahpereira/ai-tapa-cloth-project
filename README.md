# ai-tapa-cloth-project

### AI for the Arts and Humanities (B)

This project explores the relationship between generative AI, digitisation, and cultural heritage through the study of *Sp Coll Hunterian K.5.22*, Alexander Shaw’s 1787 catalogue of tapa cloth samples collected during the voyages of Captain Cook.
It investigates whether AI can meaningfully interpret culturally significant archival textiles, or whether digitisation reduces them to aesthetic surface patterns. It uses image analysis, AI-generated outputs, and human interpretation, the project examines questions of authenticity, colonial collecting, materiality, and archival representation.
